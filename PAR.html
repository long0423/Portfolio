SUMMARY

I re-designed UI and added new functionality on PAR dashboard system and was in charge of the front-end coding.

Problem Statement

PAR (Pistol Attendance Record) is a paperless dashboard system which is integrated with the PAR mobile app. The combination of those is designed for pistol shooters to eliminate the hassle of filling out forms when participating shooting events at pistol clubs within states.

The goal is to make it easier for users to keep and manage shooting records. Our customers already has a very basic version of the dashboard and app, so we need to re-beautify and include more functionality to the system.

Project Goals

Redesign of current dashboard system to accommodate more features

A more helpful tool to assist police to manage registered shooters' records

Optimise the registration and login for users from different regions

Improve the user experience of searching and generating records

Introduce mobile and tablet optimisation

APPROACH

To gather insights on the current PAR experience from pistol shooters, club staff and police, with a focus on both long term and short term states.

Research Methods

Customer interviews

Market research analysis

Prototype testing

Customer journey

Persona

Information Architecture

Pistol club visits

Customer Mindsets

Pistol shooters

"I don't want to fill in repetitive information every time."

The shooter wants to keep the form-filling to a minimum and record-managing to a maximum.

Will switch between app & dashboard and seek feedback to make sure what they are getting is exactly right.

The police

"I need to manage all non-compliant records of all shooters."

The police needs to track the shooting records of all shooters at the clubs within the state.

The system needs to generate a detailed forms of all non-compliant records that shooters did not complete throughout the year.

PROTOTYPING

We set up a hi-fi clickable prototype to help aid the customer conversations and also provide usability insights.

Enabling New Functionality

Through close collaboration with the lead developer, we were able to build several new functionality requested by end users on top of system optimization, and conducted feature test on both dev and user side.

Pixel Consistency

Alongside the finished interface designs, I produced a guide file documenting all elements to keep the designs as consistent and re-usable as possible. This was an important tool for the development team to use and build an understanding for.

Learnings

Seek critical feedback early

Despite the close collaboration between design and build, there could have been more honest and practical conversations had early on when defining the blue print. Our ambition blinded us early, which resulted in inadequate time for build feedback and a premature release.
