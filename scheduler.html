SUMMARY

I created the web application prototype and lead UI design and be a part of the front-end coding

Problem Statement

What our client wanted is a personally customized web application that meets the client's daily work schedule. In view of the already existing scheduler applications on the market, our client hopes that this customized application simplifies the operation steps for end users and removes useless and cumbersome functions as in others with a flexibility for future function expansion.

Given that most end users of our client are middle-aged and elderly workers, both of our dev team and design team had to be careful to make all functionalities and procedures simplified and intuitional because some of them don't even know how to use a smartphone which brings a real challange when it comes to system design.

Project Goals

Simplify application logic & reduce operation steps

Build connection between the host end and the mobile end

Bring "drag and drop" functionality to job scheduling

Capacity and flexibility for future functionality expansion

Differentiate from competitors through innovation

Customer Mindsets

Job managers

Scheduling various roadworks every day is a complex and repetitive task. Each worker needs to be considered and coordinated to ensure that the work is completed on time. So what we need is a simple application that minimizes repetitive inputs.

Also it's important to have such features as to search and manage documents of all the records that we completed and had issues in.

Crew members

What we need is an easy way to view the daily schedule and receive work assignment information in a very simple way. At the same time, many of us workers don't use smartphones, so don't show off with your high-tech operation, it's important to be able to organize work in the dumbest way possible.

INTERACTION

We optimized all the modals for job creation and assignment and improved the drag and drop interaction after several attempts to help aid the end users to get a smooth experience.

Also we try to make the job info page as simple and clear as possible when users click the link in text messages to accept or decline the job that has been assigned to them.

Optimize Notification

Through constant discussion and feedback from end users, we decided on two optional notification methods for users to accept or reject the job assignment, SMS link and email. Together with a minimal design and sharp color contrast, the content will be as obvious as possible.

Pixel Consistency

Alongside the finished interface designs, I produced a guide file documenting all elements to keep the designs as consistent and re-usable as possible. This was an important tool for the development team to use and build an understanding for.

Learnings

Be aware of the final scope

Be aware of how far the system will go and make sure to set the final scope of the application in advance, because most users do not think systematically about system design, but just ask for functionalities that pop into their heads. Sometimes there can be inconsistencies when analyzing the function carefully. Therefore, it is necessary to rank the importance of the functions in order to avoid conflicts with the previous completed design when the functions are appended later.
