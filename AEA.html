SUMMARY

I lead design on the fully customized Educational App with a purpose to achieve paperless teaching environment

Problem Statement

AEA (Australian Education Academy) is a member of Victoria Registered Training Organization. They offer a wide range of flexible training programs. Our customer wanted our help to launch their own educational App to reduce the mass of paper files produced during the teaching process and to integrate student information, course information, class schedules, course materials and even student services into this mobile App.

With a request to serve both the students and teachers (or administrators), this App would allow different roles of users to access to control and interact with the whole course ecosystem, all from the customer's smartphone.

Core User Needs

Information is key

Whether a student chooses one course or several courses, displaying the curriculum and materials required for the courses is the key features of the app, which was originally the intention to replace the paper-based information carrier.

Without traditional paper files to browse, it's important that the app can service these core users needs.

I don't need paper any more

The app should be a great replacement for the paper materials generated during the teaching process including handouts, reading materials, and various application forms used in student services such as course registries and transcript applications. Even classroom tests can be done in this app.

Displaying what I need

In addition to displaying information that can be displayed on paper, the app should also offer features such as showing course progress, reminding of goals and tasks, and participating in online comments and course discussions

It better be worth the space

Users are protective of their storage space and regularly cull apps they don't use often.

We had users conduct a card sorting exercise to help us understand what is important to them in an app experience.

Following our customer interviews, we synthesized our findings and were able to create a customer journey and persona which were presented and discussed with the client.

These exercises formed a great foundation to move into feature ideation and prioritisation.

Course lists

This section displays all the enrolled courses with progression indicator and other users who enrolled in the same course for discussion purpose. Course detail screens display course info including timetable and interactive comments with other students.

Resources management

Users can download all files provided by enrolled courses from different categories. Indicator displays the current status or progress of files to be downloaded or already downloaded. Users can view files online or locally using file reader app.

Student services

Online application submission such as certificate/transcript application. Users can fill in the forms with signature and submit for any application enrolment or online quizzes.

News update

The bulletin board section allows users to browse the recent news and update from school with push notification and join discussion with other users.

Learnings

Our customer

We were able to get regular updates from customer support and analytics, but weren't able to gather important qualitative feedback. This made it difficult to help our client make evidence based decisions with ongoing features. This isn't an uncommon issue, and wasn't solved from simply raising these issues in meetings. Running small workshops to discuss feature desirability of customers could have been an effective way to expose important unknowns and encourage directional research.

Out of sight and out of mind

The introduction of design systems within our workflow was a relatively new aspect of our process and I had underestimated the success of its adoption. A design system needs constant reference, particularly when operating within teams that see regular change in personnel. A design system's success relies on a dedicated advocate.
