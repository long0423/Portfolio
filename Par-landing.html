SUMMARY

I re-designed the one-page marketing promotion website of PAR and the UI of PAR app.

Problem Statement

Our client has a basic version of PAR (Pistol Attendance Record) app and a dashboard management system that integrates with the app. We've asked to re-create the landing page to promote the download of the app and registration of the dashboard.

This re-design brings a clearer and more user-friendly layout with proper illustration instead of displaying slides of the app screenshots. Also we try with a mild color system to create a sense of trust and profession.

Pain Points Resolution

A clean and tidy webpage that illustrates the app and web portal

Highlight the practical key features of the app and dashboard

Re-design the page with a eye-friendly color system that goes with the app

Create a close connection between the app and dashboard system

Learnings

When a website redesign goes right

When we redesigned PAR landing page, we started by running a dedicated research round looking specifically at the qualitative, four-pillar 'I want to know, I want to go, I want to do, I want to buy' framework.

We collected a lot of data on the questions people had, how they were going through the site, where their anxieties were, what were their motivations. This resulted in a lot of insight: for example, we learned that before going through the first half of the product page the benefits really need to pop out and speak to mitigating customer anxieties.
